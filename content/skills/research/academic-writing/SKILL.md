## Trigger
Use when a research task requires drafting or revising academic texts such as papers, thesis chapters, or research reports.

## Prerequisites
- Research findings or data available to write about.
- Target audience and publication venue identified.

## Steps
1. Outline the document structure following IMRaD or discipline conventions.
2. Draft each section with appropriate academic register and hedging language.
3. Integrate citations to support claims and acknowledge prior work.
4. Apply discipline-specific terminology and argumentation patterns.
5. Revise for clarity, coherence, and logical flow.

## Verification
- Document follows the expected academic structure.
- All claims are supported by citations or evidence.

## Rollback
- Restore a previous draft version.

## Common Failures
- Overclaiming results without appropriate hedging.
- Inconsistent citation style within the document.

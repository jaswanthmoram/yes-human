## Trigger
Use when a task requires drafting a research proposal for funding, thesis approval, or institutional review.

## Prerequisites
- A defined research topic and preliminary understanding of the field.
- Knowledge of the target audience (funding body, thesis committee).

## Steps
1. Define the research problem, questions, and significance of the study.
2. Conduct a preliminary literature review establishing the research gap.
3. Design the methodology section with clear procedures and justification.
4. Develop a realistic timeline with milestones and deliverables.
5. Justify resource requirements including personnel, equipment, and budget.

## Verification
- Problem statement clearly identifies the research gap.
- Methodology is feasible within proposed timeline and budget.

## Rollback
- Archive proposal drafts for reference.

## Common Failures
- Overly ambitious scope that exceeds available resources.
- Weak connection between literature review and proposed methodology.

## Trigger
Use when a research task involves analyzing qualitative data such as interview transcripts, open-ended survey responses, or observational notes.

## Prerequisites
- Qualitative data collected and transcribed.
- A defined analytical framework or openness to emergent themes.

## Steps
1. Familiarize with the data through repeated reading and note-taking.
2. Generate initial codes by systematically labeling data segments.
3. Search for themes by grouping related codes.
4. Review and refine themes for coherence and distinctiveness.
5. Define and name final themes with clear descriptions.
6. Produce an analysis report with supporting evidence quotes.

## Verification
- Themes are supported by multiple data excerpts.
- Codebook documents coding decisions and definitions.

## Rollback
- Archive intermediate coding versions for audit trail.

## Common Failures
- Imposing pre-conceived themes without grounding in data.
- Insufficient inter-coder reliability when multiple coders are used.
